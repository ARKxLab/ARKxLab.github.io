About us

Who we are?

DKU HCI Lab is led by Dr. Xin Tong at Duke Kunshan University in the department of Art and Humanity. We conduct research in the field of human-computer interaction. Our current researches focus on digital human construction, VR/AR games, VR diagnosis for psychological and physical treatment, humanitarian and inclusive system design.

Featured Projects

Twilight Rohingya

We designed a 360 VR videos-based system to depict real-life scenarios from a refugee camp with active and passive navigation. We recruited 36 participants to investigate the effects of our system on people’s implicit bias against refugees and compared how active and passive navigation may differ in reducing those biases.

Learn more

Virtual Human for ASD

The study aims to use virtual humans to teach children with autism to express themselves through a conversational manner.

Learn more

StarRescue

StarRescue is a two-player collaborative tablet game that aims to enhance autistic children’s social skills. It augments children’s attention paying to others, perspective-taking, and negotiation by visualizing the turn-taking collaboration process.

Learn more

Privacy for Families with Autism

This study explores the privacy issues among families with autism by interviewing parents of children with autism. It collects perceptions and concerns of families with autism in their daily lives and information exchange online and offline,and the protection behaviors they have taken.

Learn more

Measuring Anxiety

We use machine learning algorithms to measure, predict and design interventions for stress and anxiety. To detect, access and intervene with such disorders is crucial for the wellbeing of the general population, traditional self-assessments for mental health have certain limitations due to their subject nature.

Learn more

Entangled Habitation

We explore the entangled habitation design concept to advocate a more-than-human perspective in human-nature interaction design. Following the entangled habitation design concept, we implemented three plant-based wearables to reframe human’s interactions with plants.
